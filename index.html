Le Lo La stuff

Pronouns

Nominative Dative Accusative
yo me me
tu te te
el/ella/usted le lo/la/lo
nosotros/nosotras nos nos
vosotros/vosotras os os
ellos/ellas/ustedes les los/las

An Accusative case is the one that uses transitive verbs
aka both subject are required for it to work
"I'm washing my car" - you can't wash nothing,
a recipient, the car, is required for "washing" to mean something
n.b.: you can also be washing yourself, but mentioning ****self makes a reflexive form and thus no accusative case

I am going to acuse him.
Lo voy a acusar.

A Dative case is the one that uses ditransitive verbs
aka subject, object and indirect object are required for it to work
"I'm not giving you my address" - you need something to give/show/tell to the recipient, even if it's "it", "this" or "that" pronoun

I am going to give him. (verbose - I am going to give IT to him)
[se] Le voy a dar. (n.b. "se" is implied)
